## Key Takeaways

1. **Two coaching modes exist on a spectrum:** performance coaching (task-focused) and development coaching (person-focused). Neither is inherently wrong.

2. **Default under pressure is performance:** When stakes are high, most people jump in with solutions, tighten control, and take ownership of the thinking — leaving the other person feeling unaccomplished.

3. **Development coaching is not passive:** It's "staying curious long enough for the other person to grow." It means resisting the urge to rescue, holding space through silence, and helping someone think rather than telling them what to do.

4. **Practical technique:** Before offering a solution, ask *"What have you already considered?"* then pause. Capability is often already there, waiting to be invited forward.

---

## Key Quotes

> "Performance coaching focuses on the task at hand. Development coaching focuses on the person doing the task."

> "It's staying curious long enough for the other person to grow."

> "It's asking a question and resisting the urge to rescue."

> "You might be surprised at how often capability is already there, just waiting to be invited forward."
