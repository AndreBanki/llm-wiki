## Key Takeaways

1. **The "good host" role goes beyond logistics.** A good host intentionally creates conditions for great people to meet, think, and create together — not just gathers them in a room.

2. **Five is the ideal number for a deep single-table conversation.** MBS hosted a dinner of five in New York; only two knew each other. The mix of familiarity and strangeness helped.

3. **Two questions unlocked three hours of conversation:**
   - *"What are you known for?"* — not public reputation; how you're known by people who care about you; an invitation to name your values and best self.
   - *"What are you up to?"* — conspiratorial; asks about the adventure beckoning, the edge you're stepping toward, who you're becoming; bypasses "business as usual."

4. **"We are the average of the five people we spend the most time with."** A small but real part of the case for deliberate community — elevating standards through proximity to the right people.

5. **Words of the year 2026 (MBS): "skipping hand-in-hand."** A vision of collaboration and joy, not just productivity.

## Key Quotes

> "One of the roles I try to inhabit is that of a good host."

> "I want great people to know other great people."

> "'What are you known for?' … It's an invitation to speak about your values and elemental parts."

> "'What are you up to?' … It's asking you to skip the 'business as usual' update and tell me what's whispering to you from the future."

> "Five people. Three hours of conversation. Two questions that unlocked some magic."
