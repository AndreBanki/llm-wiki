A PM decision model that separates customer pain discovery from solution design authority, then triangulates multiple signal streams before roadmap commitment.

---

## Core Idea

Customer signal synthesis treats customer requests as one signal channel among several, not as direct implementation instructions. It distinguishes:

- Pain signal: what users struggle with.
- Prescription signal: what users suggest should be built.

The model assumes pain signals are usually high-value while prescription signals are often bounded by current workflows.

---

## Why It Matters

Without synthesis, teams can optimize for local responsiveness while missing category-level shifts. This produces the incremental trap: many sensible roadmap decisions that collectively drift away from market relevance.

---

## Signal Stack

A robust PM synthesis loop combines:

1. Customer interviews and support tickets (stated pain)
2. Product telemetry and behavior data (revealed behavior)
3. Sales objections and win/loss narratives (commercial signal)
4. Competitive and adjacent-industry shifts (strategic signal)
5. Technology and regulatory movement (constraint/opportunity signal)

---

## Decision Rules

1. Use customer language to detect problems, not to outsource product design.
2. Require triangulation between qualitative claims and observed behavior.
3. Keep explicit capacity for non-requested strategic bets.
4. Treat repeated requests as hypotheses to test, not automatic commitments.
5. Audit interview programs for confirmation bias and narrative laundering.

---

## Common Failure Modes

- Voice-of-customer literalism: direct request to roadmap with no synthesis.
- Interview theater: research used to justify prior decisions.
- Local optimization: backlog polish while market architecture shifts.
- Channel monoculture: over-reliance on scheduled interviews and underuse of passive signal channels.
