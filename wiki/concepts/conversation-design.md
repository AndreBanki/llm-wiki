The practice of pre-designing a conversation's structure — especially its opening questions — to invite depth, bypass surface-level exchange, and create conditions for genuine connection.

---

## Definition

Conversation design, in the sense used here, is not about scripting a conversation but about choosing the **right questions in advance** to set the conditions for meaningful exchange. The designer acts as a host: creating the container, then stepping back to let the conversation move.

This is distinct from facilitation (which manages process) or coaching (which is one-on-one). Conversation design is a group hosting practice.

---

## The Good Host Role

MBS describes "good host" as a role to inhabit deliberately. A good host:

- Intentionally assembles the right people (not just anyone available)
- Creates conditions for great people to *know* other great people
- Chooses questions that invite participants to show their best, not just report status
- Holds a vision for what the conversation could *become*

The goal is "a moment of light, a flash of something created between two different people."

---

## The Two-Question Dinner Format

**Format:** 5 people, 2 questions, ~3 hours. Mix of familiar and unfamiliar participants.

### Question 1: "What are you known for?"

- **Not about:** public reputation or credentials
- **About:** how you are known by people who *care about you*
- **Function:** invitation to name values, elemental parts, and best self — possibly through accomplishments
- **Effect:** surfaces what's most important to someone, not what's most visible

### Question 2: "What are you up to?"

- **Tone:** conspiratorial — not "what's your current project?" but "what adventure is beckoning you?"
- **About:** the edge you're stepping toward; who you're *becoming*
- **Function:** bypasses "business as usual" updates; asks for what's whispering from the future
- **Effect:** people share aspirations rather than statuses

---

## Why It Works

- **Depth through constraints:** Two questions force everyone to choose what matters most
- **Safety through framing:** "What are you known for?" asks for values, not performance — lower stakes, higher authenticity
- **Forward motion:** "What are you up to?" is growth-oriented by design — it presupposes change
- **Small group dynamics:** Five people allows everyone to speak; large enough for diversity, small enough for intimacy

---

## Connection to Development Coaching

Conversation design shares the same root philosophy as development coaching (see [[coaching-modes]]): the right question, held with genuine curiosity, unlocks more than any amount of advice.

- Both resist the urge to fill space with solutions
- Both presuppose that the other person has something worth discovering
- "What are you up to?" maps closely to the development coaching question "who are they becoming?"

## Related Terms

- **Good host** — the role of the conversation designer; someone who curates people and questions to create conditions for magic
- **The Conspiracy** — MBS's accountability community; a structured context for holding Worthy Goals
- **Worthy Goal** — the aspiration a Conspiracy member commits to; the answer to "what are you up to?"
